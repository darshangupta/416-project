Car Information Visualization

Welcome to the narrative visualization of how certain factors affect vehicle performance. In this visualization, we will analyze data from a dataset containing various attributes of cars, including fuel efficiency, horsepower, and more. We'll focus on three key visualizations to uncover meaningful insights about the relationship between these factors.

Let's dive into each visualization to gain a deeper understanding of how fuel efficiency, horsepower, and other factors impact car prices and performance.

Scatter Plot: Drive Wheel vs. Horsepower

This scatter plot demonstrates the relationship between drive wheel and horsepower of cars. Each point represents a car, and its position on the plot is determined by its drive wheel (x-axis) and horsepower (y-axis).

Takeaways: We can observe how drive wheel and horsepower are correlated. Generally, rwd cars have the highest horsepower and fwd have the lowest.

Bar Chart: Average Price by Engine Location

This bar chart presents the average price of cars (y-axis) categorized by their engine location (on x-axis, either front or rear).

Takeaways: By analyzing the bar chart, we can identify that rear engine cars are generally more expensive than front engine cars.

Bubble Chart: Price vs. Horsepower vs. Curbweight

This bubble chart illustrates the relationship between curbweight (represented by the size of bubbles), Horsepower (x-axis), and Price (y-axis). The blue bubbles are front engine cars, and the orange bubbles are rear engine cars.

Takeaways: By examining the bubble chart, we can conclude that higher horsepower cars are generally more expensive. And, although a looser correlation, heavier cars are generally more expensive as well.
